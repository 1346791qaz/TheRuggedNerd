Rocky Mountain High: 10 Days in Colorado with 3 Kids & 2 Dogs

We pointed Blue west and didn't look back. Here's everything that went right — and a few things that didn't — on our longest trip yet.

🏔️

The plan was simple: load Blue, hitch up Dorey, and spend 10 days in Colorado before the kids went back to school. The execution was slightly more chaotic than the plan. That's adventure parenting for you.

We left at 4:30am on a Tuesday — because apparently I thought a pre-dawn departure would help us beat traffic. My wife reminded me that our three kids are nocturnal chaos agents and none of them slept a minute past 5am. By the time we hit the highway, the dogs were restless, Kid #2 had already asked "are we there yet?" (we were 8 minutes from home), and I was on my second travel mug of coffee.

The Route

We drove Blue north from home, picking up I-25 and heading straight for Rocky Mountain National Park. Dorey handled the 12-hour pull like a champ — new brakes make a world of difference on mountain grades, and our weight distribution hitch kept everything feeling solid even through the Colorado foothills.

"Trail Ridge Road at 12,183 feet with a 25-foot trailer in your rearview mirror hits different than any map can prepare you for."

We stayed at Moraine Park Campground for the first four nights — one of the few spots in RMNP that can fit a 25-footer without a blood sacrifice to the reservation gods. Booked these six months in advance. Do not sleep on reservations.

What Worked

The Solar Setup

Our 400W solar + 200Ah LiFePO4 battery setup ran everything except the air conditioner all day. Colorado sun in February is no joke — we were pulling a solid 18-20A through most afternoons. The kids barely noticed we were "off grid." I noticed because I was secretly geeking out the whole time.

The Dogs Were Actually Good

This was our first real mountain trip with both dogs and I was nervous. Turns out, dogs love national parks even more than children do. They were both exhausted and content every night, which meant they actually let us sleep. Life-changing development.

The Meal Prep System

We finally committed to my wife's meal prep system: 5 dinners planned and partially prepped before departure, everything labeled in the Dometic fridge. Zero "what's for dinner" arguments. This alone upgraded the trip by 40%.

What Didn't Work

Trail Ridge Road in February

Trail Ridge Road was closed. We knew this was a possibility and ignored the possibility. We stared at the closed gate for 10 minutes like it would open if we believed hard enough. It did not open.

Kid #3's Sleeping Bag

Packed the wrong sleeping bag. This is why I have a pre-departure checklist now. Specifically, a downloadable pre-departure checklist that you can use too.

Top 3 Moments

  1. Bear Lake at sunrise. Just the oldest kid and me, 7am, absolutely empty. The reflections in the water were so perfect it looked like a painting. No phones for 20 minutes. That's peak parenting.
  2. Kid #1's first solo fire-starting. We've been practicing the ferro rod for months. It finally clicked at this campsite. The pride on that kid's face was worth the entire trip.
  3. Elk herd crossing the road at dusk. About 30 elk, maybe 50 feet from the truck. All five humans and two dogs in complete, stunned silence. That's the whole point of this life right there.

Gear That Earned Its Place

  • Dometic CFX3 55 fridge — flawless, again
  • Goal Zero Yeti 1500X — kept the laptop and phones topped up without touching the trailer batteries
  • Jetboil Flash for morning coffee before the kids wake up (sacred ritual)
  • Starlink RV for evening download sessions (yes, even in RMNP)
  • Muck boots for everyone — February mud is no respecter of persons

Would We Do It Again?

Already planning the return trip for July when Trail Ridge Road is actually open. We're looking at extending to two weeks and adding a swing through southern Colorado — maybe the Great Sand Dunes and Black Canyon of the Gunnison.

If you're thinking about Colorado in a travel trailer, do it. Just book your sites early, bring chains (even in February, especially in February), and download our pre-departure checklist so you don't forget the right sleeping bag.

— The Rugged Nerd
